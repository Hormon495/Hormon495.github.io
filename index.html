Hormôn
by Caleb Mayfield, Catherine Kosek, David Warren, Rob Huddleston




Endocrinology is the branch of biology and medicine involving endocrine glands and hormones. For endocrinology students and medical professionals, understanding the underlying processes of endocrinology is far more important than memorizing terminology, but the complex jargon that describes the endocrine system can be overwhelming. As a result, high school and entry-level college students approaching endocrinology for the first time often have trouble visualizing the actual processes.


Hormôn is designed to help students learn and visualize endocrinology processes through an Android application built using Unity. Features of our game include:
  • Introductory Level travelling through the hormonal stress response process
  • Multiple scenes with locations in different organs
  • 2D side scrolling
  • Login screen
  • Audio settings
  • Keyboard or touch screen controls
  • Non-playable characters
  • Educational dialogue
  • Interactive tasks
  • Player scores based on collecting tokens
  • Death boundaries and respawn
  • Several automated test suites

Looking forward, with continued development on our game we would like to include:
  • More levels detailing additional hormonal processes
  • Refined and relevant artwork and animations
  • Glossary scene that records and defines all characters and molecules interacted with throughout gameplay
  • Augmented Reality Cutscenes
  • Google Play Player Stats
